# Killer Stats for YC Application

One-line facts with sources. Memorize these for the interview.

---

## The Problem

- **80.3%** of AI projects fail to reach production (RAND Corporation, 2024)
- **95%** of enterprise GenAI pilots deliver zero measurable ROI (MIT NANDA, 2025)
- **$547 billion** wasted globally on failed AI initiatives in 2025 (industry estimates)
- **42%** of businesses scrapped most AI initiatives in 2025, up from 17% in 2024
- **$7.2M** average sunk cost per abandoned enterprise AI initiative
- **30%** of GenAI projects abandoned after POC by end of 2025 (Gartner)
- **6-18 months** typical enterprise AI procurement cycle
- **11+ people** on a typical enterprise AI buying committee
- **$50K-$150K** cost of a single AI proof-of-concept
- Only **12%** of AI POCs reach production deployment

## The Market

- **$2.52 trillion** worldwide AI spend in 2026 (Gartner, 44% YoY growth)
- **$10.9 billion** AI agent market in 2026, growing 46% CAGR to $52B by 2030
- **$1.86 billion** AI evaluation platform market in 2025, growing 27% CAGR
- **76%** of enterprise AI is now purchased (not built in-house)
- **$644 billion** GenAI spend in 2025 (Gartner, 76% YoY)
- **4.3 million** AI-related repositories on GitHub (178% YoY growth in LLM projects)

## The Timing

- **40%** of enterprise apps will feature AI agents by end of 2026, up from <5% in 2025 (Gartner)
- **535%** YoY growth in agent frameworks with 1K+ GitHub stars (14 to 89)
- **40%+** of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027 (Gartner) — because companies can't evaluate them
- **EU AI Act** mandates pre-deployment testing with defined metrics — fully enforceable August 2, 2026
- **1,000x** drop in inference costs over 3 years
- **88%** of YC S25 batch companies are AI-native
- **$178 billion** raised by AI startups in Q1 2026 alone (Crunchbase)

## The Competition Gap

- **Zero** companies at the intersection of evaluation + competition + procurement for AI agents
- **LMArena** ($1.7B valuation) validates competitive AI eval can be huge — but evaluates models, not agent solutions
- **Kaggle** (Google-acquired) validated competition-to-hiring pipeline — but only for prediction problems, not unbounded tasks
- **CoreWeave** bought Weights & Biases for **$1.7B** (March 2025) — eval tooling is hot M&A
- **OpenAI** bought Promptfoo (March 2026) — the giants are acquiring eval infrastructure
- **Confident AI / DeepEval** (YC W25) — closest YC company, but it's internal testing, not a procurement marketplace

## Regulatory Tailwinds

- **EU AI Act** requires conformity assessments, technical documentation, and test results for high-risk AI by Aug 2026
- **NIST AI RMF** has 243 control objectives, increasingly used as procurement criteria
- **ISO 42001** (AI management) certifications up 20% YoY — Microsoft, IBM, Autodesk now certified
- US federal procurement now requires **real-world testing and independent evaluation** for AI systems

## The Agent Builder Explosion

- **89** agent frameworks with 1K+ GitHub stars (up from 14 one year ago)
- **LangChain** 126K stars, **n8n** 150K+ stars, **Dify** 136K stars
- Every major vendor shipped agent SDKs: OpenAI Agents SDK, Anthropic Agent SDK, Google ADK
- **41%** of all code is now AI-generated (GitHub Copilot data)
- **92%** of US developers use AI coding tools daily
- **25%** of YC W25 startups have 95% AI-generated codebases

## The One-Liner Stats for the Interview

When YC asks "why does this matter?":
> "80% of AI projects fail. Companies spend $50K-$150K on POCs that go nowhere. Meanwhile the agent market is growing 46% per year to $52B. There are now 89 agent frameworks and 4.3 million AI repos — but no way for a company to objectively test which agent actually works on their problem."

When they ask "why now?":
> "Two years ago there weren't enough agents to compare. One year ago there wasn't infrastructure to compare them. Now there are thousands of agents, inference costs dropped 1000x, and the EU AI Act mandates auditable evaluation by August 2026. The gap between 'agents exist' and 'companies can evaluate them' is our entire market."

When they ask "what if Google/OpenAI does this?":
> "OpenAI just bought Promptfoo for internal eval. Google owns Kaggle for data science. Neither wants to be the neutral arena where their competitors' agents get evaluated too. This has to be an independent platform — like how Gartner evaluates vendors but doesn't sell software."
